# aseme ✨

*The activating sacred power that makes a rightly-placed word accomplish what it names — divine authorization as a received, transmitted, maintained force: àṣẹ carried as a decree.*

aseme is two true roots welded from two unrelated worlds: the Yoruba **àṣẹ** (ah-SHEH), the divine power to make things happen — the word spoken at the close of a prayer not as assent but as *activation* — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So aseme is àṣẹ held as an ordinance: power that no human originates, that inheres in Olódùmarè, flows through the orisha, and is transmitted to people through initiation, lineage, and accumulated right-action. It is why the babaláwo's casting yields true guidance when the novice's identical words do nothing, why the elder's blessing carries weight, why an invocation actually lands. It survived the Middle Passage as *axé* and *aché* because it could not be translated into Western categories without loss — the untranslatable that endured because it named something indispensable.

aseme names a **face** of sacred power, not the whole of it. Where its sibling **nyamame** names the animating energy that makes the world alive, aseme names the *activating* dimension — the authorizing force that converts trained invocation into accomplished reality; nyamame answers *why is the world alive?*, aseme answers *why does this particular invocation accomplish what it names?* Its light is the dignity of legitimate, transmitted efficacy — the recognition that some words carry weight and the difference is received authorization maintained through right-living; its shadow is the channel mistaking itself for the source, hoarding the power, or wearing its costume after losing it through misconduct. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.80/10**.
